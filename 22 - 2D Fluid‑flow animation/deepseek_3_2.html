What is a Von Kármán Vortex Street?

A Von Kármán vortex street is a repeating pattern of swirling vortices caused by the unsteady separation of flow of a fluid around blunt bodies. It occurs at certain Reynolds numbers (typically Re > 40) when the flow is neither completely laminar nor fully turbulent.

This simulation uses a simplified Navier-Stokes solver (velocity-pressure formulation) to model incompressible fluid flow. The obstacle creates flow separation, leading to alternating vortices in its wake.

Drag the obstacle with your mouse to see how the vortex pattern changes in real-time!
